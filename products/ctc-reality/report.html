07 · Offer Evaluation Checklist

Before you compare two CTC numbers, check these.

A higher CTC does not automatically mean a better monthly cash outcome. Ask for the structure behind the number.

✓
Fixed annual gross

Confirm the annual amount that is fixed and does not depend on performance targets.

✓
Variable-pay conditions

Ask how the bonus is calculated, when it is paid and what percentage is typically achieved.

✓
Employer PF inside CTC

Check whether employer provident fund contributions are already included in the headline CTC.

✓
Gratuity inside CTC

Confirm whether gratuity is included even though it may not reach your bank account as monthly salary.

✓
Joining or retention bonuses

Check payment dates, eligibility conditions and any repayment or clawback clauses.

✓
Payroll deductions

Ask about insurance deductions, professional tax and other recurring payroll deductions.

08 · Questions to Ask HR

Copy these before you accept or compare an offer.

You do not need to decode the salary structure alone. Ask for the numbers that make the CTC easier to evaluate.

Could you please share the fixed annual gross excluding variable pay and one-time bonuses?

Is the employer provident fund contribution included in the stated CTC?

Is gratuity included in the CTC figure, and if so, what annual amount has been included?

How is the variable component calculated, and what percentage of the variable component was typically paid in the previous performance cycle?

Are there any monthly payroll deductions for insurance, benefits or other company programmes?

Could you share an illustrative monthly salary or payslip breakdown for this compensation structure?

09 · How This Estimate Works

The methodology behind your report.

CTC Reality starts with the annual CTC, annual variable pay or bonus and monthly provident fund estimate you entered. The variable amount is removed from the fixed annual amount used for the monthly in-hand estimate.

The current simplified tax estimate applies a ₹75,000 standard-deduction assumption and the tax calculation model used by the CTC Reality calculator. Estimated provident fund and estimated tax are then deducted from the simplified fixed annual amount.

The take-home ratio compares estimated monthly in-hand with the monthly equivalent of annual CTC. The CTC-to-cash gap is the difference between those two figures under this model.

This analysis does not know your employer's exact salary structure, tax declarations, exemptions, benefits, gratuity, insurance deductions, reimbursements or payroll policies. Use the report as an educational estimate and ask your employer for an exact compensation breakdown.

CTC Reality provides simplified educational estimates and is not payroll, tax, legal, investment or financial advice. Actual salary, tax and take-home pay depend on your salary structure, applicable tax rules, employer contributions, deductions, benefits and payroll policies. Arthiva Labs does not guarantee that the estimate will match an employer-issued payslip.
